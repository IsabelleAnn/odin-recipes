Korean Style Fried Snapper with Seasoning Sauce

The fish there is so fresh and delicious that I couldn’t resist trying out this simple, tasty, traditional Korean recipe, domi-yangnyeom-gui. It’s fried fish, but what really makes it stand out is the seasoning sauce, where the a mix of savory soy sauce, nutty sesame oil and seeds, and fresh green onion nicely compliments the fish.

Ingredients:

  • 1½ pound black snapper (or red snapper), cleaned and salted
  • 1/3 cup cooking oil
  • 2 tablespoons flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt

Seasoning Sauce

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 green chili pepper, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon Korean hot pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon roasted toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon roasted sesame seeds

Directions:

  1. Take the fish from the fridge and pat dry with a kitchen towel. Sprinkle the flour on both sides of the fish.
  2. Heat up a nonstick pan and add the cooking oil.
  3. Cook the fish until the bottom turns golden brown. It’ll take about 20 minutes over medium heat. Turn it over with a spatula and cook the other side for another 15-20 minutes until that side turns golden brown, too.
  4. Combine the seasoning sauce ingredients in a bowl.
  5. Transfer the fish to a plate and place the seasoning sauce over top of the fish.
  6. Serve with rice, kimchi, a soup or stew, and more side dishes if desired.
